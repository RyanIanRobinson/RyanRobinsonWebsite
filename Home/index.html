About Me

Hey there, legend! I'm Ryan Robinson, and if you had to pick one word to sum me up, it’d probably be “quirky” (or “weird” if we're being brutally honest). I have a bit of a reputation for waking up at the ungodly hour of 0200 on Saturdays just to catch the astronomical, nautical, and civil dawn—what can I say, I'm committed to my sunrises. Military time? Yeah, that’s a permanent setting in my life, and I’ll challenge anyone to a mental arithmetic duel any day of the week.

When I'm not busy pondering life’s mysteries or climbing the nearest mountain, you'll find me devising absolutely absurd gifts for my mates—because who doesn’t love a surprise matching shirt set or 2m teddy bear? I love math and science so now I'm also studying, reading, and coding about trading, astronomy, and health sciences.

Welcome to my little slice of the internet, where data, humour, and a whole lot of weirdness collide!

Career Journey

I studied finance in university but soon got bored of excel so I started taking adhoc Math and Computer Science courses to delve into the exciting world of numbers without dollar signs. Still with finance as my main goal, I took internships and part-time jobs to learn about Queensland tourism statistics and data visualisation, accounting software data migration, payroll, and a few less entertaining things.

Leaving university, I'm here starting my journey at KPMG. I began in a Analytics and Data Management team doing predominantly data governance, management, and visualistion. I was pulled into a financial advisory team but quickly got bored and started doing work for the AI division. I've been learning NLP and implementing scoring analytics using decision trees, knowledge graphs, and exploring neural networks right now. KPMG has been a place where I've lead teams of analysts, driven the strategy of AI projects, budgeted software tools, and sparked my interest in creating coding projects like this website.

I don't know what the next stop for me is, but I'm hoping it's something that will fit one of the projects I do on this website!
